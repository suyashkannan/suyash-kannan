### **Regional Patterns of Weather**

---

#### 1. Weather vs. Climate

- Weather: Short-term atmospheric conditions (temperature, wind, precipitation) over hours, days, or weeks.
    
- Climate: Long-term average of weather patterns over decades or centuries.
    
- Key Difference: Climate is what you expect; weather is what you get.
    

---

#### 2. Causes of Rainfall/Precipitation

- Mechanism: Air cools as it rises, causing water vapor to condense into clouds, rain, or snow.
    
- Processes Causing Air to Rise:
    

1. Surface Heating: Common in tropical regions near the equator.
    
2. Orographic Lift: Air rises when it encounters mountains.
    
3. Weather Fronts: Collision of two air masses (e.g., cold and warm fronts).
    

- Condensation Nuclei: Tiny particles (e.g., dust, smoke, sea salts) necessary for water vapor to condense.
    

---

#### 3. Types of Precipitation

1. Seasonal Rain:
    

- Driven by shifts in solar heating and evaporation.
    
- Example: Monsoons in India and Bangladesh (June–September).
    

1. Frontal Weather:
    

- Occurs at the boundary of two air masses (cold or warm fronts).
    
- Cold fronts cause rapid uplift of warm air, leading to thunderstorms.
    
- Warm fronts create broad bands of clouds and prolonged drizzle.
    

1. Cyclonic Storms:
    

- Large wind systems rotating around a low-pressure area.
    
- Examples: Hurricanes, typhoons, and tropical cyclones.
    

---

#### 4. Seasonal Rain and Monsoons

- Monsoon Definition: Seasonal reversal of winds due to shifts in solar insolation and temperature gradients between land and ocean.
    
- Mechanism:
    

- Hot land surfaces create convection currents, lifting moist air and causing heavy rainfall.
    
- Example: Indian monsoon (June–September) supported by the Himalayas.
    

- Impact of Climate Change:
    

- Monsoons are becoming more unpredictable, intense, and destructive.
    
- Warmer air holds more moisture, leading to heavier downpours and flash floods.
    

---

#### 5. Frontal Systems

- Cold Fronts:
    

- Cooler air pushes under warmer air, causing rapid uplift and thunderstorms.
    
- Narrow bands of precipitation, followed by cooler temperatures.
    

- Warm Fronts:
    

- Warmer air slides over cooler air, creating broad bands of clouds and prolonged drizzle.
    
- Example: Cirrus clouds extending 1,000 km ahead of the front.
    

---

#### 6. Cyclonic Storms

- Formation:
    

- Develop over warm ocean waters (>26.5°C).
    
- Rising air releases latent heat, intensifying the storm.
    
- Rotate counterclockwise in the Northern Hemisphere and clockwise in the Southern Hemisphere.
    

- Impact:
    

- Cause extensive damage through high winds, heavy rainfall, and storm surges.
    
- Examples: Hurricane Katrina (2005), Super Typhoon Haiyan (2013).
    

- Effect of Global Warming:
    

- Warmer oceans provide more energy, making cyclones stronger and more frequent.
    
- Example: Hurricane Dorian (2019) rapidly intensified into a Category 5 storm.
    

---

#### 7. Tornadoes

- Formation:
    

- Small-scale cyclonic storms formed from thunderstorm energy.
    
- Occur where cold fronts collide with warm, humid air (e.g., U.S. central plains).
    

- Characteristics:
    

- Extremely intense winds (up to 340 km/h).
    
- Most common in spring and summer.
    

- Impact: Destructive in localized areas, capable of lifting houses and causing severe damage.
    

---

#### 8. Jet Streams

- Definition: Fast-flowing air currents in the upper atmosphere.
    
- Role:
    

- Deflect weather systems and storms.
    
- Stronger in winter due to steep temperature gradients between poles and mid-latitudes.
    

- Impact of Climate Change:
    

- Melting sea ice and warmer Arctic temperatures weaken jet streams.
    
- Leads to more sinusoidal (wavy) patterns, causing extreme weather events (e.g., Arctic blasts in Texas).
    

---

#### 9. Recent Extreme Weather Events

- January 2019 Cold Air Outbreak: Record-breaking low temperatures in the U.S. Midwest and Northeast.
    
- Clyde River Blizzard Events (2021/22): 33 blizzard days with extreme winds.
    
- "Beast from the East" (2018): Severe cold weather across Europe and North America due to polar vortex displacement.
