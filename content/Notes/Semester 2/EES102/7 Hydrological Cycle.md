### **Hydrological Cycle**

---

#### **1. Overview**

- **Total Water on Earth**: ~1,386 million km³.
    
- **Distribution**:
    
    - **Oceans**: 97.5% (saline water).
        
    - **Freshwater**: 2.5% (~35 million km³).
        

---

#### **2. Water Cycle Processes**

1. **Evaporation**:
    
    - **Total Evaporation**: ~496,000 km³/year.
        
        - **Ocean**: 86% (~426,560 km³/year).
            
        - **Land**: 14% (~69,440 km³/year).
            
2. **Precipitation**:
    
    - **Ocean**: 90% of evaporated water falls back as rain (~383,904 km³/year).
        
    - **Land**: 10% carried to continents (~42,656 km³/year).
        
        - **Total Continental Precipitation**: ~111,000 km³/year (61% from land evapotranspiration).
            
3. **Runoff and Groundwater**:
    
    - **Renewable Supply**: Precipitation (111,000 km³) - Evaporation (69,440 km³) = 41,560 km³/year.
        
    - **Residence Times**:
        
        - **Atmosphere**: ~9 days.
            
        - **Soil Moisture**: Weeks to months.
            
        - **Rivers**: Weeks to months.
            
        - **Lakes**: 10-100 years.
            
        - **Groundwater**: 200–10,000 years.
            
        - **Oceans**: ~3,200 years.

### **Carbon Cycle**

---

#### **1. Overview**

- **Definition**: Movement of carbon among Earth’s spheres (atmosphere, biosphere, oceans, geosphere).
    
- **Reservoirs**:
    
    - **Ocean**: Largest reservoir.
        
    - **Geologic Reserves**: Fossil fuels.
        
    - **Terrestrial Surface**: Plants and soil.
        
    - **Atmosphere**: CO₂.
        

---

#### **2. Carbon Fluxes**

- **Photosynthesis**: CO₂ uptake by plants.
    
- **Respiration**: CO₂ release by organisms.
    
- **Slow Processes**:
    
    - Fossil fuel formation (millions of years).
        
    - Calcium carbonate deposition (shells, corals).
        
    - Volcanic activity releases carbon.
        

---

#### **3. Carbon Sinks and Sources**

- **Sinks**:
    
    - **Photosynthesis**: Forests, oceans.
        
    - **Soil and Wetlands**: Organic matter accumulation.
        
- **Sources**:
    
    - **Respiration**: Cellular respiration.
        
    - **Combustion**: Fossil fuel burning.
        
    - **Anthropogenic Activities**: Deforestation, soil degradation.
        

---

#### **4. Keeling Curve**

- **Definition**: Long-term record of atmospheric CO₂ measurements at Mauna Loa Observatory.
    
- **Significance**: Shows increasing CO₂ levels due to human activities.

### **Nitrogen Cycle**

---

#### **1. Overview**

- **Importance**: Essential for proteins, DNA, and enzymes.
    
- **Nitrogen Fixation**:
    
    - **Process**: N₂ → NH₃ (ammonia) or NH₄⁺ (ammonium) by bacteria.
        
    - **Key Bacteria**: Rhizobium, Azotobacter.
        

---

#### **2. Processes**

1. **Nitrification**:
    
    - NH₃ → NO₂⁻ (nitrite) → NO₃⁻ (nitrate) by soil bacteria.
        
2. **Assimilation**:
    
    - Plants absorb NO₃⁻ or NH₄⁺ to form amino acids.
        
3. **Denitrification**:
    
    - NO₃⁻ → N₂ or N₂O by bacteria in low-oxygen conditions.
        

---

#### **3. Anthropogenic Impact**

- **Fertilizers**: Synthetic NH₃ production.
    
- **Fossil Fuel Burning**: Releases N₂O (a greenhouse gas).
    
- **Eutrophication**: Excess nitrogen in water bodies leads to algal blooms and hypoxia.
    

---

### **Phosphorus Cycle**

---

#### **1. Overview**

- **Importance**: Essential for cells (DNA, ATP).
    
- **Cycle**: One-way path from rocks → soil → plants → animals → water → ocean.
    

---

#### **2. Processes**

1. **Weathering**: Rocks release phosphate ions (PO₄³⁻).
    
2. **Absorption**: Plants take up phosphate from soil.
    
3. **Decomposition**: Decomposers return phosphorus to soil.
    
4. **Runoff**: Phosphorus flows into water bodies, leading to eutrophication.
    

---

#### **3. Anthropogenic Impact**

- **Fertilizers**: Increase phosphorus in ecosystems.
    
- **Eutrophication**: Algal blooms and hypoxia in water bodies.
    

---

### **Hypoxia in the Gulf of Mexico**

---

#### **1. Causes**

- **Excess Nutrients**: Nitrogen and phosphorus from agricultural runoff.
    
- **Algal Blooms**: Explosive growth of algae.
    
- **Decomposition**: Bacteria consume oxygen, leading to hypoxia (<2 ppm O₂).
    

---

#### **2. Impact**

- **Economic Loss**: $250–450 million/year in fisheries.
    
- **Ecological Damage**: Fish kills, loss of biodiversity.
    

---

#### **3. Solutions**

- **Reduce Fertilizer Use**: Limit nutrient runoff.
    
- **Restore Wetlands**: Natural filtration of nutrients.
    

---

### **Residence Time of Water and GHGs**

---

#### **1. Water**

- **Ocean**: ~3,136 years.
    
- **Atmosphere**: ~9 days.
    

---

#### **2. Greenhouse Gases (GHGs)**

- **CO₂**: ~100 years.
    
- **CH₄**: ~12 years.
    
- **N₂O**: ~114 years.
