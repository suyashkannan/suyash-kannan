### **Matter, Energy, and Life**

---

#### **1. Building Blocks of Matter**

- **Elements**: Made up of atoms; the basic units of matter.
    
- **Organic Compounds**: Carbon-based compounds that form the foundation of life.
    
- **Matter**: Anything that takes up space and has mass; exists in four states—solid, liquid, gas, and plasma.
    
- **Conservation of Matter**: Matter is neither created nor destroyed; it is recycled.
    

---

#### **2. Atoms, Molecules, and Compounds**

- **Molecules**: Formed by similar types of atoms (e.g., O₂).
    
- **Compounds**: Formed by different types of atoms (e.g., H₂O).
    
- **Chemical Bonds**:
    
    - **Ionic Bonds**: Transfer of electrons (e.g., NaCl).
        
    - **Covalent Bonds**: Sharing of electrons (e.g., DNA, proteins).
        

---

#### **3. Acids and Bases**

- **Acids**: Substances that release H⁺ ions in water (e.g., HCl).
    
    - **Environmental Impact**: Acid rain, ocean acidification, acid mine drainage.
        
- **Bases**: Substances that bond with H⁺ ions (e.g., NaOH).
    
    - **Role in Life**: Essential for metabolic activities.
        

---

### **Energy for Life**

---

#### **1. Laws of Thermodynamics**

1. **First Law (Conservation of Energy)**: Energy is neither created nor destroyed; it transforms from one form to another.
    
2. **Second Law**: Energy conversions result in some energy being lost as low-quality heat.
    

---

#### **2. Forms of Energy**

- **Kinetic Energy**: Energy of motion.
    
- **Potential Energy**: Stored energy.
    
- **Chemical Energy**: Energy stored in bonds (e.g., glucose).
    
- **Heat Energy**: Low-quality energy released during energy conversions.
    

---

#### **3. Energy in Ecosystems**

- **Source**: Sunlight (45% visible, 45% infrared, 10% ultraviolet).
    
- **Photosynthesis**: Converts light energy into chemical energy (glucose).
    
    - **Efficiency**: Only 1-2% of sunlight is captured by plants.
        
    - **Process**:
        
        - Light-dependent reactions: Split water into O₂ and H⁺.
            
        - Light-independent reactions: Use ATP and NADPH to produce glucose.
            
- **Cellular Respiration**: Releases energy by breaking down glucose into CO₂ and H₂O.
    
- **Net Primary Productivity (NPP)**: Rate of biomass accumulation; depends on temperature and water availability.
    

---

#### **4. Chemosynthesis**

- **Definition**: Process by which organisms use chemical energy (instead of sunlight) to produce food.
    
- **Examples**:
    
    - Deep-sea hydrothermal vents (e.g., black smokers).
        
    - Hot springs (e.g., Yellowstone National Park).
        
    - Extremophiles: Organisms like _Pyrolobus fumarii_ survive at 113°C.
        

---

### **Ecosystems**

---

#### **1. Definition**

- **Ecosystem**: A biological community and its physical environment.
    
    - **Abiotic Factors**: Nonliving components (e.g., climate, water, sunlight).
        
    - **Biotic Factors**: Living organisms and their effects.
        

---

#### **2. Trophic Levels**

- **Producers**: Photosynthetic organisms (e.g., plants, algae).
    
- **Consumers**:
    
    - **Herbivores**: Plant eaters.
        
    - **Carnivores**: Flesh eaters.
        
    - **Omnivores**: Eat both plants and animals.
        
- **Scavengers**: Clean up dead remains (e.g., vultures).
    
- **Detritivores**: Consume detritus (e.g., ants, beetles).
    
- **Decomposers**: Break down organic materials (e.g., fungi, bacteria).
    

---

#### **3. Food Webs**

- **Definition**: Interconnected food chains showing energy flow.
    
- **Energy Pyramid**:
    
    - Energy degrades at each trophic level (10% rule).
        
    - Example: 100 kg of clover → 10 kg of rabbit → 1 kg of fox.

### **Carbon Footprint of Diets**

---

#### **1. Emission Factors (kg CO₂e/kg)**

- **Beef**: 27
    
- **Pork**: 12
    
- **Chicken**: 6.9
    
- **Fish**: 6
    
- **Dairy**: 3.1
    
- **Eggs**: 4.5
    
- **Grains, legumes, fruits, vegetables**: ~0.5–2
    

---

#### **2. Diets and Carbon Footprints**

1. **Meat-Based Diet**:
    
    - **Daily Emissions**: 9.17 kg CO₂e/day.
        
    - **Annual Footprint**: 3,346 kg CO₂e/year.
        
2. **Vegetarian Diet**:
    
    - **Daily Emissions**: 1.47 kg CO₂e/day.
        
    - **Annual Footprint**: 537 kg CO₂e/year.
        
3. **Vegan Diet**:
    
    - **Daily Emissions**: 0.5 kg CO₂e/day.
        
    - **Annual Footprint**: 183 kg CO₂e/year.
        

---

#### **3. Global Impact**

- **Meat Eaters (73% of 7.8 billion)**: 19.1 trillion kg CO₂e/year.
    
- **Vegetarians (5% of 7.8 billion)**: 209.4 billion kg CO₂e/year.
    
- **Vegans (1.5% of 7.8 billion)**: 21.4 billion kg CO₂e/year.
    

---

#### **4. Reducing Carbon Emissions**

- **Shifting to Vegetarian Diet**: Reduces emissions by ~2.8 trillion kg CO₂e/year.
    
- **Shifting to Vegan Diet**: Reduces emissions by ~18.9 trillion kg CO₂e/year.
