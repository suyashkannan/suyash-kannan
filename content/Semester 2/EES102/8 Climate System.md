### **Climate System**

---

#### **1. Main Segments of the Climate System**

1. **Atmosphere**: Layer of gases surrounding Earth.
    
2. **Hydrosphere**: All water on Earth (oceans, lakes, rivers, etc.).
    
3. **Lithosphere**: Earth’s solid outer layer (crust and upper mantle).
    
4. **Biosphere**: All living organisms and their interactions.
    
5. **Cryosphere**: Ice and permafrost.
    

---

### **Atmosphere**

---

#### **1. Composition of the Atmosphere**

- **Nitrogen (N₂)**: 78% – Essential for plant growth.
    
- **Oxygen (O₂)**: 21% – Required for respiration.
    
- **Argon (Ar)**: 0.93% – Inert gas.
    
- **Carbon Dioxide (CO₂)**: 0.04% – Key for photosynthesis and greenhouse effect.
    
- **Water Vapor (H₂O)**: Variable (0-4%) – Influences weather and climate.
    
- **Other Gases**: Neon, helium, methane, ozone, hydrogen.
    
- **Aerosols**: Tiny particles that affect energy distribution.
    

---

#### **2. Layers of the Atmosphere**

1. **Troposphere**:
    
    - **Altitude**: 0–12 km.
        
    - **Features**: Contains 75-80% of atmospheric mass; zone of weather.
        
    - **Jet Streams**: Fast-flowing air currents influencing weather.
        
2. **Stratosphere**:
    
    - **Altitude**: 12–50 km.
        
    - **Features**: Contains the ozone layer; temperature increases with altitude.
        
    - **Polar Stratospheric Clouds (PSCs)**: Form at very low temperatures.
        
3. **Mesosphere**:
    
    - **Altitude**: 50–80 km.
        
    - **Features**: Coldest layer; burns meteors.
        
4. **Thermosphere**:
    
    - **Altitude**: 80–500 km.
        
    - **Features**: Contains the ionosphere; temperatures up to 2,000°C.
        
5. **Exosphere**:
    
    - **Altitude**: 600+ km.
        
    - **Features**: Outermost layer; gases escape into space.

### **Earth’s Energy Budget**

---

#### **1. Incoming Solar Radiation**

- **Reflection**: ~30% of solar energy is reflected by clouds, aerosols, and Earth’s surface.
    
- **Albedo**: Reflectivity of Earth’s surface and clouds; planetary albedo is ~0.30–0.35.
    

---

#### **2. Greenhouse Effect**

- **Process**:
    
    - Earth absorbs solar energy and re-emits it as infrared radiation.
        
    - Greenhouse gases (CO₂, H₂O, CH₄, N₂O, O₃) trap this heat, warming the atmosphere.
        
- **Impact**: Without greenhouse gases, Earth’s average temperature would be ~−18°C.
    

---

#### **3. Energy Imbalance and Climate Change**

- **Current Imbalance**: Greenhouse gases are trapping more energy, leading to global warming.
    
- **Ice-Albedo Feedback Loop**: Melting ice reduces albedo, exposing darker surfaces that absorb more heat.
    
- **IPCC Report**: Energy imbalance increased from 0.60 W/m² (20th century) to 0.79 W/m² (2006–2018).
