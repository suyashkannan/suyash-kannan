### **Ion Exchange Chromatography**
#### **Anion and Cation Exchange**
- **Materials**: Polymer beads with charged functional groups
- **Process**:
  - Protein mixture added to column
  - **Elution buffers**:
    - Low salt buffer: Separates by charge
    - High salt buffer: Displaces bound proteins
- **Elution order**:
  - Cations with large positive charge elute last
  - Anions with large negative charge elute first

### **Electrophoresis: SDS-PAGE**
#### **Apparatus Setup**
- **Components**:
  - Stacking gel (larger pore size)
  - Resolving gel (smaller pore size)
  - Glass plates and spacers
- **Process**:
  - Proteins migrate toward anode (+)
  - Separation by molecular weight:
    - Small proteins move faster
    - Large proteins move slower

#### **SDS-PAGE Principles**
- SDS (sodium dodecyl sulfate) masks intrinsic protein charge
- Proteins migrate based on mass under electric field
- **Gel Recipes**:
  - Resolving gel: Neutral pH
  - Stacking gel: Low pH

### **Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR)**
#### **Overview**
- Invented by **Kary Mullis** (1983)
- Nobel Prize in Chemistry (1993, shared with Michael Smith)
- **Applications**: Gene cloning, disease diagnosis, forensics

#### **PCR Components**
- DNA sample
- Primers (forward/reverse)
- Nucleotides (dNTPs)
- Taq polymerase
- Buffer
- Thermal cycler

#### **PCR Steps**
1. **Denaturation**: 94-95°C (DNA strands separate)
2. **Annealing**: 50-56°C (primers bind)
3. **Extension**: 72°C (new strand synthesis)

#### **Exponential Amplification**
| PCR Cycles | DNA Copies |
|------------|------------|
| 1          | 2          |
| 2          | 4          |
| 3          | 8          |
| 5          | 32         |
| 10         | 1,024      |
| 20         | 1,048,576  |
| 30         | ~1.07 billion |

### **Primer Design Example**
**Template DNA**:  
5’ CTTATTAGTTTACTATAAAGGAGTCGAAGAGAAGTACCAAAAGAT 3’  
3’ GAATAATCAAATGATATTTCCCTCAGCTTTCTCTTCATGGTTTCTA 5’  

**Primers**:  
- Forward: `5’ CTTATTAGTTTACTAT 3’`  
- Reverse: `5’ ATCTTTGGTACTTCTC 3’`
