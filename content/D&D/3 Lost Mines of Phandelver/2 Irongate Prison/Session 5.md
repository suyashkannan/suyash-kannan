## The Bluelords

He graduated from the Academy in the year 1418, and has led many troops into war with the [[Pirates of the West]] and against the [[Wyrms of Airem]]. He has received this title for his loyalty to the Empire and his devotion to Bemet.

#### Alarms and a Choice
A loud mechanical screeching sound resonates across all rooms and hallways, almost deafening the people. Distant footsteps and clanks of steel can be heard. Guards are approaching through their outposts. The players need to make a decision - the kitchen sewers or the wagons outside.

Give players ample opportunity to evade the chase sequence. By either getting the bluelords on their side, or by hiding somewhere.

A quick inspection of the room shows 2 glowing crystals perched atop shelves on the corners of the walls, a simple arrow could shatter the crystal, turning the alarm off. (AC = 12, HP = 2)

The first guards will take 2 rounds to arrive. The guards will arrive in parties of 3-4 after 1 round of an alarm sounding, and will chase the players. 

Traps will be activated, levers on the sides of the walls can be pulled by the guards to turn on security measures - traps.

## Door to Freedom:
#### The Loading Dock:
> The hallway leads down to a large open canopy. The iron flooring transitions into old wooden tiles, with splinters and termites populating it. The tiles creak as you run above them, crying for mercy. Up ahead, after a long week, sunlight finally graces your tired faces. The mild autumn heat and soothing breeze of wind had seemed far more distant than they once been. Freedom is now within arm's reach. The loading dock is filled with fearful wagonworkers, cowering behind their numerous wagons and trains, and three guards ready for combat, behind them horses and mules whinny and cry in fear. Crates and sacks lie around carelessly, as if someone dropped them right in their tracks and ran away. Beyond the canopy, large evergreen trees attempt to mask the setting sun, and rolling hills that seem to go on forever. 

The wagonworkers here are part of a wagon train called '[[Blue Trading Company]]', apparently sanctioned by the King himself to transport goods across the continent. There are 8 wagonworkers and 2 wagonmasters here along with 6 wagons. The wagonmasters are Fritz Hansen and Andreas Brandt. These people are cowering in fear, behind the guards.

- The crates in the room contain clothes, food, ingredients, weapons and other supplies being transported into the prison.
- The sacks contain rations and other food items for the guards.
- The wagons also contain hidden chests that have gold and jewellery inside.

The horses are tethered to a pole behind the wagons. There are 12 horses here. The independent wagons have their horses harnessed to the wagons.

There are independent wagonmasters here as well (the warden sells his illegal goods through these people). 

- Their carts contain belongings of other prisoners that are being sold.

- The wagonworkers wouldn't agree to help the prisoners escape. But if offered a worthwhile offer, they might change their mind. Since they've witnessed the players murder guards, they would not believe in any lies they have concocted.

## Sewers and Mice:
#### Kitchen Reeks of Death:
The players enter to find Chad beating one of his gnome workers, and the others sit scared. 

The door to the sewers is right beneath the washing area, and any of these people will tell them with too much prodding. If Chad finds out the players are attempting to leave through the sewers, he will attempt to go along with them. The sewers however require a key to access. But a DC17 STR check can just uproot the trapdoor off of its hinges entirely.

The key to sewers are hanging on Koustav's keychain containing multiple keys. 

As soon as the trap is opened:
> Your nose scrunches up and your mouth twists into a frown as a stench so foul that it could kill runs up your nose. The stink of rot, of death and of Khraag. Looking inside the trapdoor, you peer down into a shaft containing nothing but shadows. You hear streams of flowing sludge and slurry beneath the shadows. 

The tunnel is 80ft long. The players probably have ropes and torches by now. Falling from that height can result in 80d6 bludgeoning damage. Instant death basically. If a player is brave enough/dumb enough to jump down directly, have them make an acrobatics check DC17 to prevent them from falling flat on their face and dying and taking 4d6 damage instead.
#### The Maze:
### Third Tunnel
> The shaft leads into the third sewer tunnel, marked by wet signboards on the walls. Water and sludge is being carried through this tunnel in both directions and from the top, more sludge is splashing into the centre. Two concrete platforms on either side of the water are covered in moss and look uncared for. The stench is unbearable. Water is splashing in every direction, and some even lands on your clothes - staining them in green and yellow. 

Have them make a perception check - DC 13 - notice the squeaking of mice and rats scurrying around, carrying food.  
- There's feces and sludge in the water, and even chopped limbs and rags. 
- There's rubble in both corners of the room, where water had deposited them. 
- A DC14 Inv check reveals - an amulet worth 100gp, a dagger, 3 broken arrows, a sliver-lined wallet belonging to a merchant worth 50gp and a small blob of slime. 

The rats here are wererats, suspicious of intruders, they will follow the players till the very end.
If the players inspect the rats closely, attribute one distinct feature to a rat, and then the players will be able to figure out that that specific rat has been following them.
