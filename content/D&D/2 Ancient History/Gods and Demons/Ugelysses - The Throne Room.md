>"Arise, gods of the Ugelysses, your thrones beckon you. The fate of the worlds lie in your hands." - inscriptions on the Board

*The Ugelysses* or the Divine Throne Room in [[Lang Celestial]] - is one of the [[Heavenly Planes]]. The [[Pantheon|Gods]] of the [[Higher Planes]] each have a [[Divine Throne]], surrounding a long silver table - named [[The Board]].

The plane is surrounded by nothingness. Plain white nothingness that extends to infinity. The Board itself seems to go on and on. The table is silver in colour, with gold inscriptions and filigree in celestial. The legs of the table too far away from any point to be seen. 

The table has 7 thrones on either side. Each throne a little different from the other, representing what domains each of the gods govern. The thrones are in the order:

| Col 1     | Col 2   |
| --------- | ------- |
| Zeit      | Machala |
| Merla     | Vivirel |
| Khorjiga  | Bemet   |
| Premael   | Khraag  |
| Yora      | Eretza  |
| Crystofer | Empty   |
| Sahelis   | Kartoz  |

The thrones are huge, the splat (backrest) extending up to nothingness, with massive stumps for arm-rests, each bearing symbols and insignia. 

Atop the thrones, once every few centuries in [[The Flawless Lands]], the gods assemble in the Ugelysses, to discuss matters regarding the proceedings of [[The Mesh of Fate]] weaved by [[00 Zeit, the Fate-Weaver]]. Any anomalies, problems and suggestions are put forth by the gods and judged by [[The Board]], who happens to be sentient. 

## The Gods:
The gods, beings as tall as the infinite nothingness, seated upon their thrones, silent in deep thought. [[The Board]] begins by presenting the first motion of the Ugelysses. The gods argue, fight and even wage war over these motions - [[List of Ancient Wars]]. 
## Judgement
> Arise, Bemet of Truth. The divine justice of thy scale lies unquestioned. Thou shall judge, and the unworthy shall repent.

[[09 Bemet of Truth]] arises from his throne, and the board reveals the [[Auburn Scales of Truth]], that seem to emerge from the table itself. Bemet then raises his hairy arm, and his palm is placed upon on the scales. The other scale holds the object being judged - god, mortal or inanimate - none shall be barred from Bemet's justice. The scales adjust under Bemet's hand until they reach equilibrium - justice. If Bemet's hand lies above the object - guilty, and if below - innocent. Bemet decides the punishment and it is enacted - with no opposition.
