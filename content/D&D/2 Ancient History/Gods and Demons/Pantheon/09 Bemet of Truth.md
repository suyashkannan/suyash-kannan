1. **Bemet** (M) - Eons ago, when man had just begun taking his first steps, the Dwarves were busy forging divine weapons, and establishing impregnable fortresses. From the hottest of forges emerged a child - Bemet Forgeborn - he possessed a terrifying presence. Despite being a mere child, none could hide their intentions from him, and for some reason, lying to him felt impossible. Truth seemed to squirm its way out of the throat, like a spider crawling its way out. A beacon of truth, righteousness and justice - he went on to establish the Forgeborn clan, and forged, using his own blood and bones - a warhammer. The Warhammer of Bemet is a clan heirloom and is the most safeguarded treasure of the Forgeborn clan. He ascended to godhood at the ripe age of 20, having defeated the Warha, the Tyrant Slaver who terrorized the continent at the time.
    
    - Domain: Truth, Justice, Blacksmith
    - Symbol: _A crimson tinted glass warhammer, pointing upwards._
    - Alignment: N
    - Worship:
        - Worshipped in certain towns and cities, generally by Dwarves, Blacksmiths and Weaponsmiths; many members of the nobility follow and worship this deity as well.
            
        - **Place of worship**: Monasteries of Bemet are found only in Dwarven Fortresses. Each monastery houses a dozen monks, who live and study there. The monks swear an oath during their initiation ceremony:
            
            - "I swear, with my hand upon my throat:  
                This flesh, callused and scarred, shall never give breath to falsehood.  
                No lie shall pass my lips, no deception take root within me.  
                The path of truth shall be the path I walk, unbroken,  
                Should I falter, let my head fall from my shoulders and my body burn upon the forge.  
                And let thereafter the ashes bear the forgiveness i have bartered."
            
            "I swear, with my hands upon my chest:  
            To shield the righteous and strike down the corrupt.  
            To grant mercy where mercy is earned, and let my fury consume the wicked.  
            Justice shall be my banner, honor my armor, and righteousness my blade.  
            May the halls of my ancestors never welcome me, should I falter in my duty.  
            I swear devotion to Thy sacred grace, now and forevermore."
            
            - "Ah sweir, wi’ ma haund upon ma thrapple:  
                This flesh, cooried an’ scarred, shall ne’er gie breath tae fausehood.  
                Nae lee shall pass ma lips, nae guile tak root within me.  
                The path o’ truth shall be the road ah walk, unbroken.  
                Should ah faulter, let ma heid fa’ frae ma shouthers an’ ma corp burn upon the forge.  
                An’ let thereafter the ashes carry the pardon ah hae bartered."
            
            "Ah sweir, wi’ ma haunds upon ma breest:  
            Tae shield the richtfu’ an’ strike doon the ill-trickit.  
            Tae gie mercy whaur mercy is earned, an’ let ma fury burn the wicked tae ash.  
            Justice shall be ma banner, honour ma armour, an’ righteousness ma blade.  
            May the halls o’ ma forebears ne’er welcome me, should ah faulter in ma duty.  
            Ah sweir devotion tae Thy sacred grace, noo an’ forevermair. - _The Oath of the Bemet Monastery, Translated from Dwarvish by Ruck Forgeborn_
            
        - There aren't many religious texts for this deity, and if there are, they are kept inside the monastery, unavailable to the common folk. Many hymns exist for this deity - seeking forgiveness for lying, for committing a crime, etc.
            
        - The monks are bound to their words - if a lie escapes their mouth - consciously or not - at that very instant - their heads explode, and their beheaded corpse turns to ashes. Bemet as a deity despises people who lie and break oaths. Death is only punishment they deserve. His followers agree with this idea, and embrace his divine punishment, as long as they are forgiven - a barter coming from deep devotion.
            
        - Many of the common folk and nobility use Bemet as a means for moral laundering. Committing crimes in the day, seeking forgiveness in the night, only to repeat this cycle.
            
            - There exists a famous folk story prevalent in the dwarf communities - "A crooked thief who steals at night, and asks for forgiveness during the day. Bemet, amused at this character, attempts to teach him a lesson.........." - _Bemet's Thief, a folk story_
        - **Rituals**: To acquire the blessing of Bemet before beginning a new piece, blacksmiths engrave their sledgehammers with a simplified symbol of Bemet.
            
        - **Treasure**: The Warhammer of Bemet - A 5 foot long giant warhammer, that appears as if made of glass. The entire hammer is tinted with crimson, as if painted with blood. The hammer is a large blocky cuboid with its outer face studded with iron canines. The claw at its behind it curves downward, and takes a sharp turn upwards - giving it a bull-horn like appearance. The long handle is draped with leather, with its tapered end laced with red rope. The side faces of the warhammer are lettered with Dwarvish runes that imbue it with magic. This warhammer is kept safely inside the dungeons beneath the Forgeborn Fortress, and is brought out for the public to awe at it and try their hand at lifting it during the Spring Festival. The warhammer is said be almost as heavy as an elephant, and only those who have not spoken a lie in their life are able to lift it. This prevents thieves from even attempting to steal this divine weapon. It was lifted by an Elf ages ago, even before the great war, and hasn't been lifted ever since.
            
        - **Secret**: none
            
        - **True Form**: A Dwarf, so large, that mountains pale in comparison. Long flowy hair cover his round head, shadowing his eyes and face. Behind the rope-thick strands of brown dusty hair, two orbs of yellow shine through, long horizontal slits inside the orbs, like the eyes of a goat seem to pierce through mind and soul. A long shaggy beard flows down to his hip, braided messily. Four giant arms grip a ginormous glass warhammer. From the shoulder down the tips of his fingers, brown fur-like hair cloaks his forelimbs. His enormous body is supported by two mountain sized legs, covered in hair.
