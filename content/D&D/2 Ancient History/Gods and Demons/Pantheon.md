The continent of Airem worships 13 deities as 'Higher Deities', and worships numerous other local deities and saints as 'Lower Deities'. Deities in this world are extremely powerful beings, who have grown careless and lethargic. Some deities still respond to prayers, but unless a being is favored immensely by the gods, their prayers remain unheard. Due to the nature of religion, peoples' belief has not yet diminished, they continue to hope for their prayers to be heard, and their wishes to come true.

## Higher Deities
1. **The Great Hero/Bluebearer**  (M) - The unnamed hero who valiantly fought and defeated the tyrant dragon Ignatius. The first emperor of the Bluebanner Empire was a confidant of his. Known only through the memoirs of King Anax I and the divine visions of the clergy anointed by the empire, and very little is known about this deity. Yet, he remains the most popular and widely worshipped. Almost every living creature owes its life to the Bluebearer. Raising the Blue Banner across the continent, he fought Ignatius single handedly, felling him in a single, swift strike; while his armies fought valiantly against the evil forces of Ignatius.
	- Domain: Victory, Swords and Prosperity
	- Symbol: *A blue banner and a broadsword forming a cross.*
	- Alignment: LG
	- Worship:
		- Temples all across Airem, in almost every village and town. 
		- Religious texts (2) - *Rise of the Bluebearer, King Anax Maximus I; Hymns to the Bluebearer, Father Basu*.
		- **Hymns:**
		
			- "I bow before thy unyielding gaze, O eternal king of kings,
			And kneel true before the might of thy blade gilded and unyielding.
			I summon thee not, in mortal folly, for I am but a speck in your radiance, 
			Yet I beseech thee, in reverence servile, for hope of light ever brighter.
			Unsheathe thy blade and stand again as shield to thy humble servant,
			For thy banner be my sanctuary, and thy name my salvation." - *Hymn of Protection, verse 22-23, Hymns to the Bluebearer*
			
			- "Thy benevolence is boundless, thy virtue unfaltering as flame,
			seeketh I thy divine magnanimity for the cause of mine sin is naught but mine own.
			Sinneth I with mind unclouded, heart tainted, mine virtue shaded from thy grace divine,
			Yet now in humility, I beg for redemption, for thou art the redeemer of all mankind.
			Shine forth thy light and enlighten the eclipsed judgement of thy servant most humble,
			For thy banner be my sanctuary, and thy name my salvation." - *Hymn for forgiveness, verse 37-37, Hymns to the Bluebearer*
		
		- Has the most number of followers among the Higher Deities, generally farmers, soldiers, and common folk. 
		- People of Airem have an unusual attachment to this idea of a Great Hero. Any non-believer is sentenced immediately to the [[Irongate Prison]] in the capital of the empire, Askovia. Usually, they are executed a week or so later. Speaking ill of the Hero is one of the [[9 Unforgivable Sins]] in the constitution of the empire.
		- Every year, during the onset of the spring season - The capital issues a notice to all villages, cities, and outposts - The four day long holiday - [[The Spring Festival]], has begun! This festival honors the battle between the hero and the dragon, and celebrates the victory of good over evil. The deity of Spring - Viviel is also worshipped on the first and last day, while the 2nd and 3rd days are dedicated to the hero. 
		- **Place of worship**: The venerable clergy anointed by the great hero himself, have established sects, and churches all across the continent. Any town, village, city would have at least a small church dedicated to the hero. 
		- **Rituals:** Chanting the Hymns - for protection, power, wealth, forgiveness etc. Reading the stories of the great hero is said to provide immense courage and strength - which is why the armies of the empire have the priests recite and conduct the chants and rituals given in 'Rise of the Bluebearer' before marching for war. 
		- **Treasure**: The broadsword used by the Great Hero is passed down from one king to another - it symbolizes the transfer of power, wisdom, and strength. The broadsword -  a shimmering blade made of speckless silver, with a golden hilt studded with ochre and crimson jewels. A fluted grip allowed for comfortable wielding, as fingers slipped into their natural position. A small rusty iron chain connected to the pommel, onto which hangs the symbol of Mother Airem (read below). It is believed that the divine blade possess mystical qualities, imbuing the wielder with terrifying powers. Numerous people have attempted to thieve and acquire this power, but have failed time and time again. The treasury carefully guards this weapon and ensures that it does not fall into wrong hands.
		- **Secret**: The Great Hero does not exist. All of the stories and accounts are myths. A secret that only a few know of, and all others who know or pretend to are sentenced to death. More about this - [[Myth of the Bluebearer]].
		- **True Form** : none

2. **Bemet** (M) - Eons ago, when man had just begun taking his first steps, the Dwarves were busy forging divine weapons, and establishing impregnable fortresses. From the hottest of forges emerged a child - Bemet Forgeborn - he possessed a terrifying presence. Despite being a mere child, none could hide their intentions from him, and for some reason, lying to him felt impossible. Truth seemed to squirm its way out of the throat, like a spider crawling its way out of a narrow pipe. A beacon of truth, righteousness and justice - he went on to establish the Forgeborn clan, and forged, using his own blood and bones - a warhammer. The Warhammer of Bemet is a clan heirloom and is the most safeguarded treasure of the Forgeborn clan. He ascended to godhood at the ripe age of 20, having defeated the Warha, the Tyrant Slaver who terrorized the continent at the time.
	- Domain: Truth, Justice, Blacksmith
	- Symbol: *A crimson tinted glass warhammer, pointing upwards.*
	- Alignment: N
	- Worship:
		- Worshipped in certain towns and cities, generally by Dwarves, Blacksmiths and Weaponsmiths; many members of the nobility follow and worship this deity as well. 
		- **Place of worship**: Monasteries of Bemet are found only in Dwarven Fortresses. Each monastery houses a dozen monks, who live and study there. The monks swear an oath during their initiation ceremony:
			- "I swear, with my hand upon my throat:
			This flesh, callused and scarred, shall never give breath to falsehood.
			No lie shall pass my lips, no deception take root within me.
			The path of truth shall be the path I walk, unbroken,
			Should I falter, let my head fall from my shoulders and my body burn upon the forge.
			And let thereafter the ashes bear the forgiveness i have bartered."
			
			"I swear, with my hands upon my chest:
			To shield the righteous and strike down the corrupt.
			To grant mercy where mercy is earned, and let my fury consume the wicked.
			Justice shall be my banner, honor my armor, and righteousness my blade.
			May the halls of my ancestors never welcome me, should I falter in my duty.
			I swear devotion to Thy sacred grace, now and forevermore."
			
			- "Ah sweir, wi’ ma haund upon ma thrapple:
			This flesh, cooried an’ scarred, shall ne’er gie breath tae fausehood.
			Nae lee shall pass ma lips, nae guile tak root within me.
			The path o’ truth shall be the road ah walk, unbroken.
			Should ah faulter, let ma heid fa’ frae ma shouthers an’ ma corp burn upon the forge.
			An’ let thereafter the ashes carry the pardon ah hae bartered."
			
			"Ah sweir, wi’ ma haunds upon ma breest:
			Tae shield the richtfu’ an’ strike doon the ill-trickit.
			Tae gie mercy whaur mercy is earned, an’ let ma fury burn the wicked tae ash.
			Justice shall be ma banner, honour ma armour, an’ righteousness ma blade.
			May the halls o’ ma forebears ne’er welcome me, should ah faulter in ma duty.
			Ah sweir devotion tae Thy sacred grace, noo an’ forevermair. - *The Oath of the Bemet Monastery, Translated from Dwarvish by Ruck Forgeborn*
			
		- There aren't many religious texts for this deity, and if there are, they are kept inside the monastery, unavailable to the common folk. Many hymns exist for this deity - seeking forgiveness for lying, for committing a crime, etc.
		- The monks are bound to their words - if a lie escapes their mouth - consciously or not - at that very instant - their heads explode, and their beheaded corpse turns to ashes. Bemet as a deity despises people who lie and break oaths. Death is only punishment they deserve. His followers agree with this idea, and embrace his divine punishment, as long as they are forgiven - a barter coming from deep devotion.
		- Many of the common folk and nobility use Bemet as a means for moral laundering. Committing crimes in the day, seeking forgiveness in the night, only to repeat this cycle.
			- There exists a famous folk story prevalent in the dwarf communities - "A crooked thief who steals at night, and asks for forgiveness during the day. Bemet, amused at this character, attempts to teach him a lesson.........." - *Bemet's Thief, a folk story*
		- **Rituals**: To acquire the blessing of Bemet before beginning a new piece, blacksmiths engrave their sledgehammers with a simplified symbol of Bemet. 
		- **Treasure**: The Warhammer of Bemet - A 5 foot long giant warhammer, that appears as if made of glass. The entire hammer is tinted with crimson, as if painted with blood. The hammer is a large blocky cuboid with its outer face studded with iron canines. The claw at its behind it curves downward, and takes a sharp turn upwards - giving it a bull-horn like appearance. The long handle is draped with leather, with its tapered end laced with red rope. The side faces of the warhammer are lettered with Dwarvish runes that imbue it with magic. This warhammer is kept safely inside the dungeons beneath the Forgeborn Fortress, and is brought out for the public to awe at it and try their hand at lifting it during the Spring Festival. The warhammer is said be almost as heavy as an elephant, and only those who have not spoken a lie in their life are able to lift it. This prevents thieves from even attempting to steal this divine weapon. It was lifted by an Elf ages ago, even before the great war, and hasn't been lifted ever since. 
		- **Secret**: none
		- **True Form**: A Dwarf, so large, that mountains pale in comparison. Long flowy hair cover his round head, shadowing his eyes and face. Behind the rope-thick strands of brown dusty hair, two orbs of yellow shine through, long horizontal slits inside the orbs, like the eyes of a goat seem to pierce through mind and soul.  A long shaggy beard flows down to his hip, braided messily. Four giant arms grip a ginormous glass warhammer. From the shoulder down the tips of his fingers, brown fur-like hair cloaks his forelimbs. His enormous body is supported by two mountain sized legs, covered in hair. 

3. **Saintess Yora** (F) -  A deity venerated for her unwavering devotion, piety and tireless perseverance towards Mother Airem (Eretza). Favored immensely by Eretza, she became a living vessel of the divine. She performed miracles with ease: curing incurable diseases with a mere touch, breathing life into the dying, and even turning water into healing elixirs. Her popularity grew, drawing the old, the poor and the sick from across the continent, and people from far and wide visited her. She welcomed all, and sent no one away empty handed. She remained celibate all her life, dedicating every moment to worshipping Mother Airem, and renouncing all worldly attachments, devoted solely to fulfilling her divine duty. When she was on her deathbed, it is believed that Eretza herself guided her, hand in hand - like mother and daughter - to her heavenly abode.
	 - Domain: Life, Health and Piety
	 - Symbol: *An inverted drop of holy water, hanging from a beaded necklace.*
	 - Alignment: CG
	 - **Worship**:
		 - Religious texts: *Teachings of St. Yora, Adren Galanodel; Healing Chants by St. Yora, St. Yora*
			 
			 -  "O Saintess Yora, daughter of Mother Airem,
				I beseech thee: protect thy son,
				for he hath suffered long,
				Thou didst not let the sick depart unwell,
				Why then must he suffer beneath thy sacred fane?
				Cleanse thy son of his affliction - ==alleviate pain==" - *St. Galanodel's spell -  Healing word*
		 
		 - Worshipped by doctors, nurses and clerics belonging to the life domain. The commonfolk and nobility flood her temples during times of plague and disease. 
	- **Rituals/Culture**: During the process of birth giving, midwives often carry a beaded necklace, made of wood, an inverted drop shaped chunk of silver hanging from it. The midwives call upon the Saintess and put the necklace on the woman. This is said to ensure a painless and effortless delivery. Doctors often pray before administering patients, and seek forgiveness when death snatches the lives of their patients away. Her clerics follow in her footsteps. They become celibate, renounce material pleasures, and leave their families behind. They were a recognisable habit: A white shirt/tunic upon which a black robe is draped, wooden sandals, and a beaded necklace bearing the symbol of Saintess Yora. The men wear skull caps, while the women wear a coif. They travel from temple to temple, offering medicine and cure to the ailing. The members of this sect denounce violence. A few members of the sect alone are exempted from this rule, resorting to violence only for self defense. 
	- **Treasure**: While the Saintess never had any possessions, she wore a simple beaded necklace, which she had made with her hands. Hanging from which was a solidified drop of holy water. This necklace is now in possession of the St. Galanodel Church in the forest city of Elefsaria. The only the priests and nuns are allowed to touch and view the exhibit. This necklace doesn't have any special qualities.
	- **True Form**: A beaded necklace, with the wooden beads as large as moons, forms a circle around an inverted drop of holy water, magically holding its place in air. A huge pale thumb positioned where a limb would be, twitches. The other fingers extend and form a fist, covering the giant droplet. The huge fingers slowly unfurl with holy water dripping from the fingers, splashing on the earth, flooding rivers and lakes. A single massive foot beneath the circle lifts itself and stomps on the ground, sending tremors across the earth. Large river like veins decorate the foot, purple in colour, a stark contrast to the lifeless pale foot. On the droplet, two ethereal faces float side by side. One weeps for her ailing and dying devotees, while the laughs and smiles, not at their pain and sickness, but for the mercy to come.
