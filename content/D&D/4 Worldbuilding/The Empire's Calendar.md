## Time Reckoning System: 
- Developed by the Bluebanner Empire, designed during the early reign of King Anax
- The calendar has travelled across the continent and almost all major civilisations use the same calendar, with slightly altered names. 
- **Year** - 360 days + 4 days of the Spring Festival 
- B.R - Before Rise, A.R - After Rise.
- **Months** - 13, named after the Higher Deities ([[Pantheon]]) - each contains 28 days
- **Weeks** - 60 weeks - each contains 6 days
- **Festival Days** - 4 days devoted to the gods - Bluebearer and Viviel - Celebrating harvest and victory. Do not belong to any month.
- **Leap System** - Every three years a leap week of 6 days is added, along with 1 extra festival day - celebrating a special solar eclipse.

## Months of the Year:
1. **Bemethar** - Named after Bemet; Bemet Monasteries all across the continent celebrate the birth of Bemet (13th-14th of this month) - [[Bemet's Monastery]]

2. **Merlan** - Named after Merla. 

3. **Yormay** - Named after Saintess Yora; Clerics and Paladins of Yora begin their year-long journey on the last yorday of this month. For the year to come, they journey from city to city, helping the ailing and the poor - [[Yormay Expedition]].

4. **Karth** - Named after Kartoz; As the temples of Kartoz are no longer worshipped, this month is more or less uneventful. In Halfling valleys however, this month is a month of laughter and mischief. People play pranks on each other, indulge in feasts, and celebrate laughter. 

5. **Vivirel** - Named after Viviel; Worshipped widely by elves and druids, they conduct several rituals in their forests to please the deity. It is believed that visiting his forest shrines would grant a good harvest. For this reason, farmers and their wives visit these forests in large numbers. - [[Shrine of Elefsaria]]. The countdown for the Spring festival begins during the last week of the month - [[The Spring Festival]].

6. **Skymarch** - Named after the Great Hero; Preparations for the spring festival last for the entirety of this month. The festival is celebrated for 4 days, between the months of Skymarch and Sahelor. This month is considered most auspicious for almost all new tasks. Towns and cities see great development during this month. The clergy and the churches of the Skymarcher begin rituals and initiation ceremonies in accordance with the dates of the festival. The farmers begin their harvest on the first day of the spring festival.

7. **Sahelor** - Named after Sahelis; This month is generally eventless and is mostly a wrap up of the spring festival. During the last week of this month, the empire celebrates its soldiers and martyrs. Matriarchal civilisations across the continent celebrate this month by organising women-only festivals.

8. **Premaire** - Named after Premael; The month of love, music and dance. Common folk await this month eagerly. The month is full of weddings, parties and events. Artists from across the continent bring their caravans to villages to display their artwork - paintings, performances, etc.

9. **Macharil** -  Named after Machala; It is believed that not worshipping Machala everyday during this month at dusk brings disease and death inside homes. People also worship Saintess Yora for protection against the evil eye of Machala. 

10. **Eretzem** - Named after Eretza; Pilgrims from all over begin their journey to the shrine of Mother Airem on the 3rd day of the month - [[Shrine of Eretza]]. Couples who have trouble conceiving also join this pilgrim. Children born in this month are considered to be blessings from Mother Airem. 

11. **Khorim** - Named after Khorjiga; This month sees a lot of nomad migration - [[Nomad Gatherings]] across the continent as winter approaches. Nomad festivals and gatherings become common. The cults of Khorjiga also become active during this month, committing crimes in the name of Khorjiga - [[Cults]].

12. **Khraal** - Named after Khraag; The Khraagfolk begin their winter festival celebrations during this month. The empire does not celebrate this month due its distaste for Khraag and Khraagfolk. 

13. **Zeire** - Named after Zeit; Mostly uneventful, except for towns and cities where Zeit is worshipped exclusively. 

## Leap Week:
While the leap week is considered no different from a regular one, the extra leap day is widely celebrated - [[Day of Union]].
