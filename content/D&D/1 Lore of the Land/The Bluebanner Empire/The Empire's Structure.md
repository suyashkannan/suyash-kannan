- The Immortal King Anax - Archduke - Duke / Marquess - Count/Earl - Baron - Knights/Bluelords/Bannerlords - Peasants/Serfs

# Levy System:
- ### Imperial Levy Law: [[Levy system]]
	- Every vassal must provide a certain % of levies from their population along with their standing army.
	- During wartime: village reeves/town bailiffs assemble peasants and serfs - the baron appoints generals (knights/bluelords) and adds his own standing army - the counts assemble the army in their mustering fields, form regiments, lead by house knights, battlemasters/commanders - the army is then sent to the duke's stronghold and then to the archduke's war camp for assembly and final merging with the standing armies of these powers.

## 1. Immortal King Anax:
- The supreme ruler of the Bluebanner empire. A devout follower of the Great Hero. 
- Owner of all land under the control of the empire.
- Duties - 
	- Assigning titles to dukes, counts and barons.
	- Giving/Revoking land and titles.
	- Conquest and War
	- Granting [[Royal Writs]] for war, mining, trade etc.
	- Using inquisitors and spies for picking out heretics, traitors and non-believers.
	- Control the [[Royal Guard]] - Assigning them tasks, missions and inquisitions.
	- The behind the scenes puppeteer of the clergy. 
	- Meetings with [[The Holy Flame and his Torches]] to discuss festivals and other events.
	- Royal Courts - The King's court considers only matters of extreme importance. Only the trials of nobles and Knights who have committed crimes take place in the presence of the King. All other trials take place in the courts of barons/counts and churches - [[Courts]]
	- Maintaining diplomatic relations with other kingdoms/empires.
	- Setting up Imperial Law and Command. Drafting/Amending the constitution of the empire.
	- Meetings with Archdukes and Dukes, and lower ranks occasionally. Also organise meetings with the Flame and his Torches. Special meetings with the King's Counsel. 
	- **[[King's Counsel]]**: 4 Ministers who offer advice and counsel to the King - Imperial Wizard, Royal Guard Commander, The Flame, The Lead Inquisitor.
	- The King also conducts a ceremonial [[Monster Hunting Tournament]] during the Spring Festival.
	- Most of these tasks are delegated to his counsel and the archdukes.

- Powers - 
	- Anything and everything can be achieved by the king through his connections - The Counsel.
	- Even if the nobles disagree with a decision - manipulation through secrets, money and religion is always possible. 

- Tropes - Refer to [[Immortal King Anax]]

## 2. The Archdukes:
- The sons of the King - 3 sons with 3 different wives - [[The Archdukes of Airem]]
- They are ranked equal to the King's Counsel.
- These princes have not yet ascended the throne - and each of them has an end goal - wealth, power, even the throne.
- They are known to be ruthless and cold-blooded, one of them being the exception.
- They command armies of around 3,000 soldiers, including mages, sorcerers and battle-masters.
- Duties:
	- In a war-like situation, they shall be positioned as commanders of the imperial forces, leading the nobles and their armies.
	- They take part in royal courts, and even lead them in the absence of the king - although they *can* be overruled by the King's Counsel
	- Each of these Archdukes oversee 1-2 dukes and collect reports/taxes/soldiers and women - as indulgent concubinage.
	- They act like immature war-lords, commanding large armies to their will and seeking pleasures and indulgences.
	- They appoint generals and commanders as well.
	- They keep the dukes in check and prevent the emergence of traitors.

- Powers: 
	- Their decisions can easily override a duke's if a conflict arises.
	- They can pressurize and exploit counts and barons, and even weak dukes. 
	- They are powerless against the clergy - the clergy is second to none (or so the people believe)
	- They have immense wealth and might - a lot of houses wish for their favor, and through them - the king's favor.
	- They are kept in check by the Royal Guard and the Inquisitors, who arrive at their doorsteps at the slightest hint of heresy or betrayal.
	- Can strip nobles of their titles, and also appoint titles - to knights, and barons, and for dukes and counts - King's approval is required.

## 3. The Dukes:
-  Kings/Leaders of independent civilisations before they were conquered/converted by King Anax. 
- Some are loyal to the king, and some to a specific archduke - but sides can change in an instant.
- They distribute their land among counts and barons. 
- Duties - 
	- Collect taxes and tithes from the counts.
	- Monitor trade routes and inter-duchy trade.
	- Send and collect conscripts.
	- Possess an army of 1000 - 2000 soldiers, and 50 - 100 Battlemasters, and a handful of Battlemages
	- Have regular meetings with the King and other dukes.
	- Organize trials for serious crimes at the ducal courts.
	- Keep the counts and barons in check - preventing them from revolting.
	- Dukes have to send tenths (tithes) to the churches
	- Responsible for monster management - prepare and send trained hunters to the plagued location. - [[Monsters in Airem]]
		- Monitor the local Wizard Circle - preparing battlemages for the king/archduke/their master and controlling mages that become too powerful. Execution of battlemages requires the verdict of Imperial Wizard/Royal Guard. - [[Punishments in the Empire]]
- Powers - 
	- They have to provide land to the church - for abbeys and temples.
	- They have to pay tithes, else they shall incur the wrath of the Bluebearer.
	- They must obey the commands of the Royal Guard, and must cooperate with any and all investigations.
	- Inquisitors can also arrive if heresy is suspected.
	- For opening mines - royal writs are required
	- Cannot usurp lands - against law
	- Starting a war with a neighboring empire - royal writ
	- Can strip titles of nobles - can appoint titles for Knights, Bluelords, Barons etc.

## 4. The Marquess:
 - Instead of Dukes, they oversee lands where tensions and the threat of war is high.
 - Controlling the counts and barons, they have a larger army - 2000-2500 soldiers, 100-120 battlemasters, 10-15 battlemages.
 - These regions could be monster infested, or simply have fragile borders.
 - Most of the powers and duties of the Marquess are similar except for - 
	 - They have to pay tithes to the church, but they are exempted from tax - they will have to provide their soldiers to the king during war though.
	 - They have to organise and monitor various schools for battle training within their march - funded by the tax collected by the Marquess.
	 - They still need writ permission for initiating war.
	 - They are considered equal in rank to a Duke, and can override Dukes during wartime.
	 - They also oversee fortified towns and cities.

## 5. Counts/Earls:
- They are the vassals of the dukes and marquesses.
- They report to their dukes/marches.
- They own armies of roughly 500 soldiers, with a few battlemages and battlemasters.
- They control a handful of barons - who report to them. 
- They generally control a few cities - manage trade routes, supervise castles, conduct monster cleansing, etc.
- Collect taxes from baronies - gold, grains, produce, etc. - and send some over to the duke and to the church.
- They also keep some of the tax for garrison pay and repairs.
- They enforce local tariffs and mining rights (with royal writs).
- Management of disasters - floods, famine, draught, etc.
- They hold county courts - trials, punishment etc. - for barons, knights, bluelords or high profile villagers. Often overseen by Judges belonging to noble families - for fair sentences.
- Assign and revoke baronial stewardship on behalf of the duke
- May commission minor churches and shrines.
- Establish and maintain market towns/cities.
- Issue temporary licenses or mercantile charters.
- Issue local tolls/taxes - but within the limit established by the duchy.


## Barons:
- Manage 7-8 villages, and a few towns.
- Manage a small castle/keep.
- Collect taxes from the Village reeves and coordinate with them.
- Building of roads, management of trade routes and markets.
- Pay tax and tithes to their higher ups.
- Conduct the baronial court - justice for peasants and villagers.
- Assign/reassign minor positions within the baron's cabinet.
- Village patrols
- Crop taxation quotas are established.

## Knights/Bluelords:
- Knights are decorated soldiers who have fought for a noble and sworn fealty to them. They are awarded with a title and some land in return.
- A Knight is the lord of a small castle or a keep, with around 10 soldiers under them. They generally control 1-2 villages.
