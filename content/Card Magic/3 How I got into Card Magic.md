It all started when my cousin taught me a card trick. It was a very simple one, one that probably wouldn't fool a lot of people, but I was amazed. I went on to perform this trick for my family and friends, and they (hopefully) enjoyed it.

Although I was caught mid-sleight multiple times, I enjoyed every moment of it. My early days into magic are the days I miss the most. I wasn't discouraged at being caught, nor was I ashamed at my inability to entertain an audience. However, it is different now. I've grown older and so has my audience. I get caught more often now, mostly because of my own lack of practice, but the humiliation is crushing.

Coming back to the story, I went on to learn more tricks from YouTube, I owe most of my skills to the excellent work of A Million Card Tricks and MLT Magic on YouTube.

I was a year into Card Magic by now and had a learning partner as well. We spent most of our time together learning sleights and flourishes. I vividly remember the struggle of learning the riffle shuffle and the cascade, it took us days to finally get it down. His presence made the learning process even more fun. 

Soon came our first big performance. My sister's birthday was fast approaching, and a small party was being planned. We sat down and created a rough script and winged the performance. It was absolutely thrilling. The adrenaline rush, the spikes of dopamine when the audience cheered, the compliments we received at the end of the performance were so so worth the hard work. 

My partner moved out a few months later, and I was busy with school work. I took a small break from magic. I occasionally watched videos online, but that was pretty much it. I had many more opportunities to showcase my magic that year and I grabbed (most of) those opportunities. This year was the last year I actively invested time into my art. 

Then came my 11th and 12th grade. 2 years of strenuous studies and schoolwork. I barely had time for cards. I practiced on holidays and vacations, but I had lost my touch. By the end of 12th grade I could barely recall any of my routines. Despite my efforts, I couldn't make it to my dream institute. I took a gap year. Another year went by and I made no progress in my card magic. This time however, I cleared the exam and got admission into my dream institute (IISER). I had a few months worth of free time before my college course started and I spent most of it reconnecting with cards. 

The Royal Road to Card Magic came into my life like a savior. Using it I re-learnt all my sleights, created new routines, and had the time of my life. 
I joined college, and showcased my talents to my new friends, although I was rusty, they thoroughly enjoyed my performances. 

Thank you for reading till the end.
