Of extant groups, crocodilians and birds bracket extinct dinosaurs,  
allowing a first order inference (Witmer 1998): If both crocodilians and birds  
possess a particular characteristic or behavior, extinct dinosaurs could share it as  
well. *A second order inference may be hypothesized when a character occurs in*  
*only one crown group*. The most unreliable inferences are those of the third order,  
based on features that neither crown group possesses.
