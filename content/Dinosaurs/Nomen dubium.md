A nomen dubium is a scientific name that is uncertain or doubtful. The term comes from New Latin and literally means "doubtful name".

Why is a name considered a nomen dubium? 

- The original specimens are lost or destroyed
- The description is not clear enough for identification
- The name-bearing type is incomplete or lacks important diagnostic features

How is a nomen dubium handled?

- In zoology and botany, a new type specimen, or neotype, can be chosen to replace a nomen dubium 

- In bacteriology, the Judicial Commission may place a nomen dubium on a list of rejected names
