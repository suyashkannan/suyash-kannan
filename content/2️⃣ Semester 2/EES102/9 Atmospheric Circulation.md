 
### **Atmospheric Circulation**

---

#### 1. Introduction to Atmospheric Circulation

- Cause: Non-uniform heating of Earth's surface due to variations in solar radiation and heat capacity differences between land and ocean.
    
- Result: Differential heating creates pressure differences, driving atmospheric circulation.
    
- Key Factor: Equator receives more solar energy than poles, leading to global energy redistribution.
    

---

#### 2. Pressure Belts and Wind Patterns

- Pressure Belts:
    

- Equatorial Low-Pressure Belt (ITCZ): Warm air rises, creating low pressure.
    
- Subtropical High-Pressure Belts: Cool air sinks, creating high pressure.
    
- Subpolar Low-Pressure Belts: Warm and cold air masses meet.
    
- Polar High-Pressure Belts: Cold, dense air sinks.
    

- Wind Patterns:
    

- Trade Winds (Easterlies): Blow from subtropical highs to equatorial lows.
    
- Westerlies: Blow from subtropical highs to subpolar lows.
    
- Polar Easterlies: Blow from polar highs to subpolar lows.
    

- Coriolis Effect: Deflects winds to the right in the Northern Hemisphere and to the left in the Southern Hemisphere.
    

---

#### 3. Vertical Energy Transport

- Convection Cells:
    

- Warm air rises at the equator, cools, and sinks at ~30° latitude (subtropical highs).
    
- Forms three major cells in each hemisphere:
    

1. Hadley Cell: Equatorial convection.
    
2. Ferrel Cell: Mid-latitude circulation.
    
3. Polar Cell: Polar circulation.
    

- Latent Heat:
    

- Energy absorbed during evaporation (2,260 J/g) and released during condensation.
    
- Drives cloud formation and precipitation.
    

---

#### 4. Intertropical Convergence Zone (ITCZ)

- Location: Near the equator.
    
- Characteristics:
    

- Trade winds converge, causing frequent thunderstorms and heavy rainfall.
    
- Home to tropical rainforests (e.g., Amazon, Congo).
    

- Precipitation: Highly variable, ranging from ~100 mm/year in deserts to 11,000 mm/year in regions like Mawsynram, India.
    

---

#### 5. Subtropical Highs and Deserts

- Formation:
    

- Warm, moist air from the ITCZ cools and sinks at ~30° latitude.
    
- Creates high-pressure zones with clear skies and dry conditions.
    

- Examples: Sahara, Atacama, and Australian deserts.
    

---

#### 6. Jet Streams

- Subtropical Jet Stream: Forms near the descending branch of the Hadley cell.
    
- Polar Jet Stream: Forms near the rising branch of the Ferrel cell.
    
- Role: Influence weather systems and storm tracks.
    

---

#### 7. Ocean Currents and Heat Redistribution

- Surface Currents:
    

- Driven by wind and the Coriolis effect.
    
- Examples: Gulf Stream (warms Europe), Kuroshio Current.
    

- Deep Ocean Currents (Thermohaline Circulation):
    

- Driven by temperature and salinity differences.
    
- Forms the "Global Conveyor Belt," redistributing heat globally.
    

- Impact of Climate Change:
    

- Melting ice and freshwater input weaken thermohaline circulation.
    
- Potential to disrupt global climate (e.g., cooling in northern Europe).
    

---

#### 8. Gulf Stream and Thermohaline Circulation

- Gulf Stream:
    

- Carries warm water from the Caribbean to northern Europe.
    
- Keeps Europe warmer than other regions at similar latitudes.
    

- Thermohaline Disruption:
    

- Freshwater from melting ice weakens deep ocean currents.
    
- Could lead to cooling in northern Europe, resembling Siberia's climate.
