*The thoughts of a man crippled from the loneliness he bought his pride with.*

I am an invisible man. Even those who see me choose not to act upon it. It isn't their fault, really. It's mine. 

I refuse to interrupt people's conversations to insert my ideas, or to follow people around like a lap dog. I would most certainly tag along, if I were invited; and place my ideas on the table, if asked to. 
But shedding my pride to do so? 
No. Even if it makes me invisible. 

What is this invisibility I speak of?
It is an ability that many dream of, but only the lonely get to suffer. My invisibility is a curse my pride has put upon me. I'm truly invisible, so much so, that people sometimes forget that I exist, even when I'm right in front of them! What a remarkable power!

This curse that I've put upon myself can be cured effortlessly. 
All I would require is the ability to shamelessly announce my existence: interrupt conversations and follow around, uninvited.
Most do this with such ease!
Oh, how they disgust me. 
Tactless, filthy fools. 
How I envy them.

There is a reason, other than pride - that prevents me from becoming one of them. 
What if my presence becomes the reason for someone's discomfort? 
Would the people I wish to be with not feel disgusted at my advances? 
I would much rather suffer from loneliness than be hated in such a manner.
A justified fear, one that even you might relate to. 

A person who shares his meaningless ideas at every chance he gets loses the respect his ideas deserve. 
I respect my ideas, and my time. 
But am I even worth the respect I demand? 
Perhaps not. 
I demand such dignity, yet I wallow in pity.

A colleague once told me, "Ideas are worthless unless shared." 
I never asked him for his insufferable advice, yet here he was, proving my stance. All his advice did was flush my respect for him down the gutter. 
But maybe his words did contain a speck of truth. 
Not that I could ever become someone like him. 

The very ideas I take pride in become chains that bind me to my loneliness. 
I am trapped in a vicious cycle. 

So I sit in pitiful silence, with just one prayer - Inject in me the audacity to become who I envy, yet somehow keep my pride intact.
A paradox perhaps.
It makes me wonder. 
Is such pride even justified? What am I truly proud of? 
Perhaps this very loneliness. 
My only companion.
