So you wish to pass through these wicked woods in the black of night? 

Beware hunters, for these woods hide beings darker than this moonless night. 

Heed my warnings, or succumb to the freaks that haunt the glades within. 

-  Vampires are the sons and daughters of the night who have risen anew from their graves, out on a hunt for blood.

	Be on the lookout for this being, marked by a dark gaunt face, sometimes marred with the blood of their recent victim, and white fangs protruding out. The bat-kin wear black cloaks and shrouds and have an odd taste for human neck. 
	Let them inside your cabin, and you'll be sucked dry, for the undead cannot trespass, they must fool you into inviting them inside. 
	Should you find yourself face to face with one - a crucifix, rosary and a wooden stake should suffice. The undead detest anything that has to do with the name of the Lord. 
	Should you carry garlic and a hand-mirror, the bat-men will keep their distance.
	Books featuring Vampires - Dracula (Bram Stoker)

Heed my warnings, hunter. Veteran you may be, but these woods enshroud mysteries far greater than what you can comprehend.

- Werewolves are the beasts that stalk the woods in the dead of the moon-lit night. Men who have now turned upon us, taken by lycanthropy. 
	
	In their human form, they roam the lands - hiding their identity, unbeknownst to us all. But the hunters of the yore have noted - in their human form, their eyes supported a furry unibrow, their finger nails curved inward - a telltale sign of their canine instincts. Cut them open, and fur shall line the inside of the wound. A weakened man in the day, a feral beast in the full-moon. 
	Some put on wolf-skin, some are born with this trait, and some are cursed by the saints - but they kill and eat, all the same. 
	How might you fend yourself, you ask? The wolf-men are vulnerable to silver. Your sword, arrows, bullets - must be lined with silver, only then will you stand a chance. But, encounter one in the dark and find your face ripped apart, for their strength lies unparalleled once they transform, and once they do - your legs shall be your best friend. Run, if you can, or find yourself welcomed as one of their own. 
	Books featuring Werewolves - Silver Bullet (Stephen King) - placeholder

- Frankenstein's monster, a freak of nature and of science - he roams these woods in search of his creator, killing all who dare cross his path.
	 
	Created ages ago as an experiment, this sentient freak has now learnt the ways of society and language. He possesses intellect far greater than our own, yet is blinded by vengeance. He is a hulk of a man, bigger and stronger than most. His yellow hued skin, pink and purple veins snaking along it, his lustrous black hair, paired with his lifeless white eyes and black lips, make him a sight to behold; and feared. 
	Fire remains his only weakness. 
	Books featuring Frankenstein's monster - Frankenstein: The Modern Prometheus (Mary Shelley)

- The Ghouls of these woods are flesh eaters who prey on children and have a greed for wealth. The Sila are their female counterpart - often pose as the long lost relatives of their new victim and taking them to their shack to devour them whole. Trust me, no long lost relative of yours will wander into these woods in the middle of the night. 
	The ghouls never stray far from their caves and shacks, and come out only to prey on their victims. They are proficient in the ways of language, albeit a little dim. Should they realise tricking you isn't feasible - they will pounce upon you. 
	And what must you do when the creatures of the subterranean attack? Use your iron sword to decapitate the being in one quick strike. And should that fail, recite the verses of the holy book - they, like all other freaks of the forest are repulsed by divinity. 
	To finish them once and for all, burn them; turn them to ashes - never to rise again.

- The Dementors are hooded shadows that thrive in the darkness offered by the towering canopies of the woods. They are the filthiest, darkest and most terrifying beings that inhabit the woods. Their skin underneath their cowl is grey and adecayed. Their mouths distort into a gaping hole that sucks out every memory of happiness from the human soul. Their kiss of death will rob the air of every emotion, sound, feeling, and hope - and leave you an empty shell, devoid of emotion. 
But that is not all, for the hooded fiends are invisible to all but wizards. You and me, we don't stand a chance. They cannot be killed, only defeated with the help of the Patronus Charm, that only a few proficient Wizards can conjure perfectly. 
Take a wizarding companion along with you in your journey, and if not, pray you never encounter one in the wild.
