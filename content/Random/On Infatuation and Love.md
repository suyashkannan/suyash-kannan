# Why are crushes so painful?
Crushes make people behave irrationally. Crushes hurt.
1. Crystallization and Fantasy:
	- The ability to imagine is a vital component in the early stages of passionate love. 
	- Crystallization - the process of idolizing someone until we are completely infatuated with them. 
		While this could hold true, *if the object of our affections truly was a perfect being*, we are essentially filling the gaps in our knowledge using whatever brilliant and attractive qualities we can think of, and falling in love with **our idea of them**, and not the person in question. 
		`"If a person keeps his mouth shut, we might think he's a philosopher."`
	- A crush is basically a 'possible' relationship, until it becomes actual.
	- Crushes occur when you know a person enough to see them everyday, but still keep them shrouded in mystery. 
	- There needs to be enough distance for imagination to begin the process of crystallization and create the image of a 'perfect person' inside your mind. 
	- When crystallization reaches its height, **we begin to believe that this 'perfect person' actually exists**. This can lead to severe disappointment, should our image be shattered. 
	- Crystallization --> Obsession
2. Second Crystallization:
	- Psychologist Esther Perel identifies that intense desire in a relationship comes a feeling of separateness from our partner, in a sense, not guaranteed to stay, *this ofc does not mean making your partner feel insecure, but rather keep a sense of separateness between the two of you*. The thrill of discovering new things about the person you love is what maintains erotic tension.
	- What cements the idea of your crystallized beloved, is the addition of doubt. The hope that our crush feels the same way about us, coupled with the fear that they might not is what solidifies our feelings. 
	- The human tendency to desire what we may not have plays a great role in this.
	- We begin to think of our beloved as someone we **may or may not have**, which makes us more attached to this 'idea' of them. 
	- We place them on pedestals, and become so concerned about what they think of us, that it drives us mad.
	- This answers the question - Why are crushes painful? 
			It is the oscillation between hope and frustration, that turns us into *mad investigators*. Investigating what our beloved thinks of us. When we think we've found evidence of their affection, we're suddenly on cloud 9, and instantly we fall flat on the ground when we are reminded of the fact that we have no chance with them.
	- This emotional rollercoaster suddenly makes everything else bland and boring.
	- When such a desire is extinguished, we are left feeling relieved, but also weirdly empty.  Suddenly, life isn't so interesting anymore.
	- Take a moment and reflect on yourself:
		- `We may simply be puppets, dancing to the tunes of hope and doubt. To what extent are we truly attracted to the object of our desires? And where have we simply become addicted to the highs and lows of this cycle?`
	- Esther Perel makes a point on Infidelity - people often find people other than their partner attractive simply because of this intersection between anxiety and hope.
3. Identity, Vanity and Ego:
	- 4 Types of love:
		- Courtly Love: formal game
		- Physical Love: sex
		- Passionate Love: what we've been looking at thus far
		- Vain Love: What we'll be looking at now.
	- Vain Love: Loving a person not for what they are, but for what their affection for say about us. 
	- ==The primary reason for finding love, is to be desired==.
	- We might not even be aware that we might be loving a person for just this reason.
	- If I were to be loved by a person I hold deep admiration for, my self esteem would most definitely go up, and my image of myself would change completely.
	- Affection is a mixture of true passion and this 'unmalicious' vanity. 
	- This also explains why a crush can be so painful. 
	- *Sometimes it is our very self-image that is on the line.*
	- There is another type of Vain Love - The Don Juan Archetype:
		- Using someone, while keeping them at an emotional distance. Someone purely instrumental in their pursuit of their love.
		- `"they are so possessed with self love that they fail to see the harm they cause, and see none but themselves as capable of joy or suffering."`
		- This will bar us completely from truly experiencing the joy of romance.
		- This is also a reminder to think twice about whoever we are falling for. While the other party might suffer the consequences in long term, we will suffer intensely.
