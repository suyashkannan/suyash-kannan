I fell in love. 
It wasn't a long time ago, two or three decades or so perhaps. 
And now that I lie on my deathbed, unable to even sit upright, I must narrate to you how I found love. 

He worked beside me. Right beside me, if memory serves. 
Ah, what a man he was! The way he jokingly teased me, the way his eyes seem to blend into mine when he talked, how his faint citrus scent carried itself to my nose - I fell in love with it all. 

We used to talk - quite a lot - while working. My attention devoted to etching his every word into my heart - and none at my work. My ringing ears can still hear his words, my wrinkled skin can still feel his touch. My useless eyes still yearn for his sight. 

Why am I telling you this? I want you to learn to love, and not to expect. Expectations lead to disappointment. Oh. I digressed. Let me go on.

What was he like, when we first met? 
A quiet man, saying just enough to get the work done. His eyes met mine, quite a few times, but they held no meaning then. 
It happened quite suddenly. 
I started to look forward to work, I started to listen for his footsteps, I began seeing him as a man. 
How I longed for him! 

We spent little time together outside of work. But it was enough, just being with him was enough. I never expected more.

Did he love me back, you ask? 
No, no. He never saw in me what I saw in him.
Let me continue.

He had won an award - for something my memory fails me for. 
I was so proud.
I ran to him. He was standing alone, perhaps fate cleared the room for us. 
Trembling, I mustered courage. 
The words swiftly left my lips - No, I dragged every last syllable out of my throat. What followed was a second of silence. Untamed, brutal silence.
I really regret not looking into his eyes when I said those words. 
What face was he making? I'll never really know.

But fate willed differently. He and I - we were never meant to be. 
What happened that day, you ask? Ah. I cannot seem to recall. 
I think I wrote it in my diary. 

Bring me my diary. 
No. I don't have time for medicine. 
I need to complete this. Bring it.

Oh. He said he didn't believe in - 
No. Never mind. Forget it. 
The lesson you need to learn, my child, is that you can find love in the most unexpected places. 
Cherish them. Love is not a transaction, don't expect to be loved back. 
Just love, freely. 
Alright. I'll take my medicine. Yes. Go bring it. 

If only I could show him the family that we had built together in my dreams - our cozy home, a table for two, and a garden full of our favorite flowers... 
No. It is foolish of me to think this way. I know him. 
He'd still look me in the eye and say just as coldly - "I don't believe in love."
